**Explain this problem using first-principles thinking. Break it down to the most fundamental truths and rebuild the explanation from the ground up.**

First-principles thinking pushes Claude to strip away assumptions, clichés and conventional wisdom. Instead of repeating the most common explanation, it has to break the problem down into its core components and rebuild the answer from the ground up. That often leads to responses that are clearer, more logical and more original.

This is especially useful when a topic feels overly complicated, full of jargon or shaped by “the way things are usually done.” By forcing the model back to fundamentals, you get an explanation that is easier to trust and easier to understand.
